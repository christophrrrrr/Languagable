# **Product Vision: Chessable for Spanish**

## **Overview**

This project is **not** another AI chatbot for language learning.

It is **not** Duolingo with AI.

It is **not** ChatGPT with a better prompt.

The goal is to build **Chessable for language learning**.

The core philosophy is that language is a skill that is mastered through **deliberate practice**, **spaced repetition**, and **guided coaching**, rather than passive study or endless conversations.

Every feature should reinforce this philosophy.

---

# **Core Philosophy**

The app exists to answer one question:

**How can someone become fluent through structured, deliberate practice instead of random conversations?**

The AI is **not** the product.

The AI is simply the engine that powers the training system.

The actual product is the learning system.

Whenever a design decision has to be made, prioritize:

* structured practice  
* measurable improvement  
* long-term memory  
* coaching  
* mastery

over "having an interesting conversation."

---

# **The Core Learning Loop**

Every learning session should follow the same cycle.

Choose a course

↓

Complete a mission

↓

Participate in a guided conversation

↓

Receive coaching

↓

Generate review cards

↓

Practice reviews over time

↓

Return to the course

↓

Improve mastery

The important part is **continuous improvement**.

The conversation itself is only one step in the process.

---

# **What the App Is**

The app is a platform where users master Spanish through interactive courses and spaced repetition.

The experience should feel much closer to Chessable than to Duolingo.

Users are training skills.

Not collecting XP.

Not completing random exercises.

Not chatting endlessly.

---

# **The Role of Courses**

Courses are the main organizational structure.

Courses represent things that people actually want to learn.

Examples:

### **Conversation Courses**

* Restaurant  
* Dating  
* Airport  
* Hotel  
* Doctor  
* Shopping  
* Friends  
* Work  
* Family  
* Traveling

---

### **Communication Skill Courses**

* Talking about the past  
* Talking about the future  
* Giving opinions  
* Making comparisons  
* Telling stories  
* Explaining reasons  
* Asking questions  
* Small talk  
* Making plans

---

### **Repair Courses**

These exist because the system detects weaknesses.

Examples:

* Por vs Para  
* Ser vs Estar  
* Articles  
* Preterite vs Imperfect  
* Pronouns  
* Subjunctive

Repair courses should **not** be the main curriculum.

They are targeted practice for specific weaknesses discovered during conversations.

---

# **Missions**

Every course consists of small missions.

A mission should usually take between 2 and 10 minutes.

Example:

Restaurant

Mission 1

* greet waiter  
* ask for a table

Mission 2

* order drinks

Mission 3

* order food

Mission 4

* ask questions

Mission 5

* pay the bill

Small missions make progress feel achievable.

---

# **Objectives**

Every mission has learning objectives.

Example:

Restaurant Mission

Objectives

* use "me gustaría"  
* use restaurant vocabulary  
* ask one follow-up question  
* use polite language

The AI should naturally guide the conversation toward these objectives.

Objectives are invisible to the learner but control the educational design.

---

# **Conversations**

Conversations are guided.

They are **not** open-ended AI chats.

Every conversation has a purpose.

The system controls:

* the scenario  
* the objectives  
* the learning goals

The AI provides the dialogue.

The structure should remain consistent while allowing enough variation to keep conversations fresh.

For example:

Restaurant conversations always involve ordering food.

But:

* menu items change  
* waiter personality changes  
* unexpected situations happen  
* follow-up questions vary

This keeps practice realistic without losing educational consistency.

---

# **AI Behavior**

The AI should behave like an excellent language coach.

It should:

* stay inside the scenario  
* encourage natural communication  
* understand multiple correct answers  
* adapt to the learner  
* challenge the learner appropriately

The AI should **not** constantly interrupt.

Instead:

* let the learner communicate  
* finish the interaction naturally  
* provide coaching afterwards

The learner should feel successful before being corrected.

---

# **Coaching**

After every conversation the learner receives coaching.

Not grades.

Not XP.

Coaching.

Example:

Today's Progress

✅ You consistently used "me gustaría."

✅ Your restaurant vocabulary is improving.

⚠️ You still confuse masculine and feminine articles.

⚠️ Try using more natural expressions instead of translating directly from English.

The feeling should be:

"I have a personal coach."

Not:

"I took a test."

---

# **Review Cards**

Every important mistake or useful expression becomes a review card.

The review system is one of the core features of the platform.

Review cards should focus on:

* mistakes  
* important vocabulary  
* useful sentence patterns  
* grammar issues  
* natural expressions

Reviews happen through spaced repetition.

Exactly like Chessable.

The app—not the AI—controls review scheduling.

---

# **Mastery**

Everything should have a mastery level.

Examples:

Restaurant

68%

Dating

42%

Talking About the Past

81%

Articles

55%

Mastery represents demonstrated ability over time.

It is never permanently completed.

Users continue strengthening skills through reviews.

---

# **Progress**

Progress should represent real-world abilities.

Instead of saying:

"You are B1."

The app should emphasize:

You can confidently:

* order food  
* introduce yourself  
* travel  
* book hotels

Currently improving:

* telling stories  
* discussing opinions  
* talking about childhood

The focus should always remain on communication.

---

# **Recommendations**

Users may freely choose courses.

However, the platform should intelligently recommend what to learn next.

Recommendations should consider:

* weaknesses  
* forgotten review cards  
* unfinished courses  
* previous performance

The platform should feel like a coach guiding the learner.

---

# **Daily Experience**

Opening the app should feel similar to Chessable.

Example:

Today's Training

* 18 Reviews  
* Continue Restaurant Mission 6  
* Recommended:  
  * Talking About the Past  
  * Articles Repair Course

The first thing users should think is:

"What should I train today?"

Not:

"What should I chat about?"

---

# **Design Philosophy**

The application should feel:

* focused  
* calm  
* professional  
* educational  
* mastery-oriented

Avoid:

* excessive gamification  
* meaningless XP  
* fake streak pressure  
* childish animations  
* random quizzes

Progress itself should be motivating.

---

# **Guiding Principles**

When implementing new features, always ask:

Does this improve deliberate practice?

Does this improve long-term retention?

Does this improve communication ability?

Does this reinforce mastery?

Does this make the learner feel coached?

If the answer is no, the feature probably does not belong.

---

# **Long-Term Vision**

The goal is to build the best platform for becoming fluent through deliberate practice.

Users should eventually feel:

"I don't just know Spanish."

"I've trained Spanish."

Exactly like a chess player doesn't simply "know openings."

They have trained them until they become automatic.

That is the long-term vision of the platform.
